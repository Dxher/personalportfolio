About Me

General Hobbies Software

I am a 22 year old graduate from the University of Ottawa. I love building stuff and staying occupied. Although Im a big bald guy and might seem unapproachable, I am very friendly. I keep a bright outlook to situations because being negative never helped anybody.

From rock climbing to sketching, I’m always exploring a new creative outlet. I love sports and the gym. I love learning when it's not graded. Knowledge is great, but I don't enjoy being tested. I began my own business and discovered my embroidering hobby. I'm in the process of merging my business and my interests of Trading Card Games and 3D printing. I enjoy gardening, traveling, and really anything life has to offer. Last but not least, I adore my rotting sessions. You need your time just to sit and do nothing.

I began coding in 6th grade. I was (still am) a fan of Pokemon and Maplestory games and decided to make a game combining the two. So at the early age of 13 (I think?), I opened YouTube and spent hours watching tutorials. That said, I was left with two incomplete games. That merger and a horror game. As I grew up, I realized I really enjoy computers and anything tech related, so in the 10th grade I took my first python and robotics course. I aced it and realized I like solving coding puzzles. From there I decided to study Software Engineering and realized there are no more puzzles :/ Never the less, it is still a fun field as I get to learn through different experiences. This can be seen in my resume under Job experiences. You'll notice I did A LOT at BAITS Global. Those deatils are not exagerated.

Automated Lighting System

Automated Lighting System

This project was completed for a embeded systems course.

We had free reign to build whatever we desired. I thought of bringing to life something I always saw in shows I thought was cool. Lights that turn on when you clap!

I quickly realized that that was a pretty easy system so I further included motion detection and bluetooth control.

This product is a solution to forgetting the lights on in multiple scenarios. For example, you just left the house and realized you left your lights on, you can now turn it on from your phone. Walked into your house with your hands full? No problem! Laying in bed comfortably and forgot to turn your lights off? Also no problem!
